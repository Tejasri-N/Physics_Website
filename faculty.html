Biography

    Dr. Anurag Tripathi earned his Ph.D. from the Harish-Chandra Research Institute, Allahabad (2009). His research lies in Theoretical Particle Physics, with a specialization in Perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) and the infrared structure of gauge field theories. He has made significant contributions to the study of scattering amplitudes, including advances in celestial amplitudes, soft theorems, resummation techniques, Feynman integrals, and multiparton webs (Cwebs). His group has introduced novel concepts such as normal ordering of Cweb diagrams, fused webs, and families of Cwebs, supported by a uniqueness theorem to understand mixing matrices.

    Before joining IIT Hyderabad, Dr. Tripathi held postdoctoral research positions at INFN-Napoli, INFN-Torino/University of Torino (Italy), University of Wuppertal (Germany), and TIFR Mumbai (India). He joined IIT Hyderabad in 2015 as Assistant Professor and has been serving as Associate Professor since October 2021.

He works at the interface of astrophysics, cosmology, and data science, with wide-ranging contributions to galaxy clusters, pulsars, gravitational waves, astrostatistics, and machine learning applications in physics. He has been a member of several international collaborations including Super-Kamiokande, LIGO, Dark Energy Survey, South Pole Telescope, and the Indian Pulsar Timing Array.

    He was awarded the 2016 Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics as part of the Super-Kamiokande Collaboration for the discovery of atmospheric neutrino oscillations, a milestone that solved the long-standing solar neutrino puzzle. Prof. Desai continues to focus on precision cosmology, pulsar timing, indirect dark matter searches, and the development of advanced data-mining techniques for astrophysics.

Biography

    Dr. Yogesh Kumar Srivastava is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad. He earned his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from the University of Lucknow, followed by an M.Tech. in Materials Science from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. He completed his Ph.D. in Photonics/Applied Physics at the School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences, Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore.

    Before joining IIT Hyderabad, Dr. Srivastava worked as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow (2019–2022) at the Center of Disruptive Photonic Technologies (CDPT), NTU Singapore, where he contributed to cutting-edge developments in photonics and metamaterials. He also gained three years of industrial experience (2011–2014) as a Materials Failure Analysis Specialist at Finisar Malaysia Sdn. Bhd., bridging applied research with technology-driven solutions.

    At IIT Hyderabad, he founded the Ultrafast Terahertz Photonics Laboratory (UTPL) in 2023, which focuses on terahertz time-domain spectroscopy, optical pump–terahertz probe techniques, micro/nano-fabrication, and numerical simulations. His current research spans ultrafast terahertz photonics, reconfigurable metamaterials, quantum materials, topological insulators, MEMS, high-Tc superconductors, sensors, chalcogenides, and THz spintronics.

    Dr. Srivastava’s interdisciplinary expertise positions him at the forefront of next-generation quantum photonic devices and terahertz technologies, with applications in both fundamental science and advanced engineering innovations.
