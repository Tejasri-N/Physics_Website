Manish K. Niranjan

Ph.D. in Physics, The University of Texas at Austin, USA

Professor and Head

Research Areas: Theoretical & computational condensed matter and solid state physics; Semiconductor and oxide heterostructures; Physics of surfaces and interfaces at atomic scale; Solar cell materials and devices; First principles design of materials, heterojunctions and nanoscale devices; Quantum transport in nanoscale devices and low dimensional systems.
Address: Room PH-308, Physics Building, IIT Hyderabad
Email: manish@phy.iith.ac.in
Phone: (040)2301-6705
Personal Website

Biography

    Prof. Manish K. Niranjan received his Ph.D. from the University of Texas at Austin, USA, and his Integrated M.Sc. from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur. His research lies in the field of theoretical and computational condensed matter physics, with a focus on electronic structure, surfaces and interfaces at the atomic scale, semiconductor and oxide heterostructures, solar cell materials and devices, and nanoscale quantum transport. He employs first-principles electronic structure methods and multiscale modeling to design and understand materials, heterojunctions, and nanoscale devices for future technologies.

    Prof. Niranjan has served at IIT Hyderabad since 2010, progressing from Assistant Professor to Associate Professor, and currently Professor & Head of the Department of Physics. Prior to joining IITH, he worked as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Nebraska Center for Nanoscience, University of Nebraska–Lincoln, USA (2007–2010)

Anurag Tripathi

Ph.D. in Physics, Harish-Chandra Research Institute.

Associate Professor

Research Areas: Theoretical Particle Physics. Perturbative QCD. Feynman Loop Integral Calculations. Soft and Collinear QCD Resummation. Higgs Phenomenology.
Address: Room PH-407, Physics Building, IIT Hyderabad
Email: tripathi@phy.iith.ac.in
Phone: 040 2301 6713
Personal Website

Biography

    Dr. Anurag Tripathi earned his Ph.D. from the Harish-Chandra Research Institute, Allahabad (2009). His research lies in Theoretical Particle Physics, with a specialization in Perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) and the infrared structure of gauge field theories. He has made significant contributions to the study of scattering amplitudes, including advances in celestial amplitudes, soft theorems, resummation techniques, Feynman integrals, and multiparton webs (Cwebs). His group has introduced novel concepts such as normal ordering of Cweb diagrams, fused webs, and families of Cwebs, supported by a uniqueness theorem to understand mixing matrices.

    Before joining IIT Hyderabad, Dr. Tripathi held postdoctoral research positions at INFN-Napoli, INFN-Torino/University of Torino (Italy), University of Wuppertal (Germany), and TIFR Mumbai (India). He joined IIT Hyderabad in 2015 as Assistant Professor and has been serving as Associate Professor since October 2021.

    Dr. Arabinda Haldar received his Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay. His research broadly falls under experimental condensed matter physics, with a focus on Spintronics, Magnonics, Nanomagnetism, and Spin Dynamics. He investigates magnetic thin films, nanostructures, and nanodevices using both experimental and simulation techniques.

    His group works extensively on magnon spintronics (magnonics), exploring spin waves as carriers of information for post-CMOS devices, and on microwave magnetics, developing reconfigurable and self-biased nanomagnets for RF/microwave applications in communication and signal processing.     Dr. Haldar has been recognized with several distinctions, including the Ramanujan Fellowship, the DAE-Young Scientist Research Award, and the Research Excellence Award by IIT Hyderabad. He also serves as an IEEE Senior Member and as an Editorial Board Member for journals such as Scientific Reports and Frontiers in Materials.

Address: Room PH-205, Physics Building, IIT Hyderabad
Email: jmohanty@phy.iith.ac.in
Phone: 040 2301 6709
Personal Website

Biography

    Prof. Jyoti Ranjan Mohanty obtained his Ph.D. from the Paul Drude Institute / Humboldt University, Berlin. His research spans a broad spectrum of nanomagnetism, ultrafast magnetism, multiferroics, magnetic microscopy, and data storage technologies, with a strong emphasis on spintronics, skyrmions, and nanoscale magnetic structures.

    His group investigates how magnetic behavior evolves when the structure size approaches characteristic length scales, which has direct applications in magnetic data storage, spintronic devices, and energy technologies. A key focus is on understanding magnetization dynamics at ultrafast timescales and nanoscale confinement, using both laboratory-based experiments and large-scale facilities such as neutron and synchrotron radiation sources.

    Prof. Mohanty has contributed significantly to magnetic imaging (scanning probe, X-ray holography, and resonant scattering), ferromagnetic semiconductors, ultrafast demagnetization, micromagnetic simulations, and 2D materials. His work has appeared in high-impact journals including Nature Physics, Nature Communications, ACS Nano, and Physical Review Letters.

Address: Room PH-203, Physics Building, IIT Hyderabad
Email: kanchana@phy.iith.ac.in
Phone: 040 2301 6702
Personal Website

Education

    Prof. V. Kanchana obtained her Ph.D. from Anna University and has over two decades of active research experience in computational condensed matter physics in India and abroad. Her research primarily focuses on exploring material properties through first-principles electronic structure calculations (Density Functional Theory – DFT). She has made significant contributions to the study of topological materials, thermoelectric materials, spintronic systems, layered compounds, and electronic topological transitions, along with investigations of material behavior under high pressure.

    Her group’s work spans a broad range of topics, including Fermi surface topology, optical and magnetic properties, lattice dynamics, phonon transport, and energy-related materials such as batteries and thermoelectrics. A key highlight of her research is addressing topological phases of matter and phenomena such as the anomalous Hall effect, Hall conductivity, and topological invariants derived from band structure analysis.

    Prof. Kanchana has been recognized with several awards and honors, including the MRSI Medal (2021), the Bronze Medal of the Society for Material Chemistry (2023), and the Young Achiever Award (2014, DAE-SSPS). She has also been elected as a Fellow of the Institute of Physics (UK), a Fellow of the Royal Society of Chemistry (FRSC, 2020), and a Member of the National Academy of Sciences (India, 2014). In addition, she serves on the Editorial Board of the Journal of Electronic Structure (IOP).
